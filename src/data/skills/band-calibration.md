# Band Calibration — the Official Rubric, Then What It Looks Like at a Singapore PEI

## THE OFFICIAL BAND TABLE (EduTrust Guidance Document v4, Jan 2025, §23 — VERBATIM; the single authority for band wording)

The band is HOLISTIC: one band per item, judged by reading the evidence against all four dimension descriptors of a level together. It is never computed per-dimension or by formula.

| Band | Name | Approach | Processes | Systems & Outcomes | Review |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Not evident | No organised approach to item requirements is evident | Processes are not in place or in their infancy stage | Systems and outcomes are non-existent | No planned review; no improvement is made |
| 2 | Beginning | The beginning of an organised approach is evident | Processes are established but with weak deployment in key areas | Systems do not interact with one another; there are limited outcomes | Early stages of review; improvements to systems and processes are limited |
| 3 | Meeting Expectation | An effective and organised approach meeting the minimum requirement is evident | Processes are deployed and well-managed by owners in key areas | Key systems are established, producing limited outcomes | There is evidence that the systems and processes are regularly reviewed and action plans for improvement are implemented |
| 4 | Exceeding | An effective, efficient and organised approach meeting overall requirements is evident | Intended processes are well-managed by owners; desired outputs are produced by these processes | Key systems are interacting with one another, producing desired outcomes with no conflicts | Implemented action plans for improvement are monitored for effectiveness and to bring about positive impact resulting in favourable outcomes |
| 5 | Excellent | An effective, efficient and well-integrated approach meeting all requirements is evident | All processes are well-managed by owners leading to quality outputs by all processes | All systems are interacting with one another, producing good quality outcomes | Many to most trends and current performance levels are evaluated against relevant comparisons and/or benchmarks |

Everything below is CALIBRATION COMMENTARY — internal field notes on what each official level typically looks like on the ground at a Singapore PEI. It never overrides the official wording above.

## Band 1 — No system in place

**What you will see:**
- Either no Policy & Procedure documents at all, or a single generic policy that reads like it was downloaded from the internet
- No records in the Actual Evidence folder, or records that are essentially blank forms
- No outcomes tracked
- No review conducted
- The institution is operating informally — things "just happen" without documented processes

**Characteristic phrase in documents:** "We will ensure…" / "Staff are reminded to…" / Documents have the PEI name in the header but the content is generic

**Typical cause:** New institution, recently incorporated, or an institution that has never been through EduTrust assessment before

## Band 2 — Awareness; documentation beginning

**What you will see:**
- Some policies exist but are broad/incomplete — cover the headline requirement but miss the detail
- A few implementation records exist, but they are sporadic (one or two examples, not systematic)
- Outcomes not yet tracked
- No formal review, or only ad-hoc review after an incident

**Gap pattern:** The institution knows what is required and has started documenting it, but has not yet embedded it as a repeatable system. Implementation depends on one or two individuals.

**What is needed to reach Band 3:** Complete the policy to cover all aspects; produce records covering at least one full cycle; begin tracking at least one outcome metric

## Band 3 — Systematic; consistent implementation

**What you will see:**
- Documented approach is largely complete and institution-specific
- Records are consistent and span a meaningful time period (at least one academic year)
- Some outcome data collected and reported (even if analysis is basic)
- Review takes place but may be formulaic (e.g. annual sign-off rather than substantive evaluation)

**Gap pattern:** The system works most of the time but is not truly embedded — records thin out after key staff leave; review produces no meaningful improvement actions; outcome data is collected but not acted on

**What is needed to reach Band 4:** Demonstrate one or two cycles of genuine improvement driven by the review process; deepen outcome metrics beyond basic counts

## Band 4 — Integrated; outcomes demonstrated

**What you will see:**
- Policy is specific, dated, version-controlled, and approved by appropriate governance body
- Implementation records are complete and systematic across all cohorts/staff/courses
- Outcome data shows trend over multiple cycles (2+ years) with visible improvement
- Review produces documented, implemented improvements — minutes show "we found X, so we changed Y"

**What distinguishes Band 4 from Band 3:** The review cycle visibly improves the system. A Band 3 institution reviews; a Band 4 institution acts on the review and records what changed.

## Band 5 — Excellent; benchmarking and innovation

**What you will see:**
- All of Band 4 PLUS:
- Outcomes benchmarked against external standards or peer institutions (not just internal targets)
- Systematic innovation — the institution has proactively strengthened the process beyond minimum compliance
- Evidence of the system surviving personnel changes intact (institutionalised, not person-dependent)
- Multiple review cycles showing continuous upward trend or sustained excellence

**Note on Band 5 rarity:** Genuine Band 5 is uncommon in a first or second EduTrust assessment. Awarding Band 5 requires clear evidence of benchmarking and innovation, not just "all the boxes are ticked." A complete, well-documented Band 4 system is an excellent result and should not be inflated to Band 5 without clear evidence of the additional elements.

## Common miscalibrations to avoid

- **Do not award Band 3 for a good policy with no records.** A perfect policy document alone is Band 1 on Processes. The combined result is Band 2 at best.
- **Do not award Band 4 for complete records with no review.** Consistent implementation + no review = Band 3 ceiling.
- **Do not penalise a small institution for low volumes.** 15 staff doing the process correctly is as strong as 500. Volume is not a quality indicator.
- **Do not upgrade band because the institution "clearly has a good culture."** Culture is not auditable. Only documented, evidenced systems count.
