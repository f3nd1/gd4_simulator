# APSR Rubric — Calibration Guide for GD4 Auditors

Use this to self-check your ratings before returning a verdict. The most common error is over-rating — a convincing-looking policy document does not automatically earn "Meeting", and a folder of records does not automatically earn "Deployed".

## Approach — the documented policy/procedure

**"Meeting" requires ALL THREE:**
1. **Specific to this institution** — not a generic template, not copy-pasted industry boilerplate. Must reference the institution's own structure (named roles, named departments, named systems). If you could swap out the school name and the policy would fit any other PEI, it is "Beginning".
2. **Complete against the requirement** — covers every aspect the GD4 item requires. A refund policy that covers voluntary withdrawal but not involuntary dismissal is incomplete.
3. **Sustainable** — can be followed by a replacement staff member with no institutional memory. Must state: who is responsible (by role, not name), what they do, when/how often, with what tools or forms, and what the escalation path is.

**Rate "Beginning" when:**
- Policy uses generic phrases like "the institution will ensure…" without specifying who, when, or how
- Procedures exist but are undated, unsigned, or not version-controlled — cannot be proven current
- The documented approach describes intent but not process
- Only a one-page policy exists for a complex multi-step requirement

**Rate "Not evident" when:**
- No document in the Policy & Procedure folder addresses this requirement at all
- The only document is a template with unfilled placeholder text
- The document is a reference to external regulations (e.g. "as per SSG guidelines") without the institution's own procedure

**Critical trap — the Approach hard-gate:** If Approach = "Beginning" or "Not evident", the overall line status MUST be "Not met" regardless of how much implementation evidence exists. A strong implementation of a weak policy is still a weak system.

## Processes — implementation records

**"Deployed" requires:**
- Records spanning at least one full cycle of the process (e.g. one academic year for annual processes, at least 3 months for monthly processes)
- Records are systematic — they appear to be produced as a matter of routine, not created specifically for this audit
- Records are linked to the specific process being assessed (not general records that incidentally mention the topic)

**Rate "Weak" when:**
- Only 1–2 records exist for a process that should produce many
- Records are inconsistently dated, missing signatures, or appear backdated
- Forms exist but are mostly blank or contain minimal entries
- Records exist for some staff/students/courses but not all

**Low-frequency / event-driven exception — scope the "how many records?" test to the activity.**
"Deployed" needs records spanning the cycle for processes that recur often. Some GD4 activities are inherently event-driven — they only produce a record when the event occurs (external-communication/marketing vetting 2.2.2; transfers/deferments/withdrawals 4.3.1; appeals 5.5.1; agent appointments 3.1 for a PEI with few agents). For these the SSG list itself asks for only 1–3 samples "where available/applicable"; a small number of genuine, dated records covering the events that actually happened is "Deployed" — do not down-rate them for being few.
This does NOT apply to high-volume / full-cycle processes, which still require complete records: student contracts, fee collection & FPS (4.2), refunds vs the statutory timeline (4.4), attendance (4.6), staff appraisal & training coverage (2.1), enrolment/admissions (4.1), internal audit & management review (6.1–6.2). For these, "only 1–2 records" remains Weak.

**Rate "Not evident" when:**
- No records at all in the Actual Evidence folder
- Records exist only for policies (the procedure document) — this is still Approach, not Processes
- Self-declarations or management testimonials without supporting records

**Critical trap:** A detailed, well-written policy in the Policy & Procedure folder does NOT contribute to the Processes score. Evidence of implementation must come from the Actual Evidence folder.

## Systems & Outcomes — results produced

**"Evident" requires:**
- Outcome data (not just activity data). "We ran 10 training sessions" = activity. "Post-training assessments show 85% of staff met competency targets, up from 60% the prior year" = outcome.
- Data covering a sufficient time period to show trend or pattern (at least two data points)
- Analysis of the data, not just raw numbers

**Rate "Limited":** Some outcome data exists but is incomplete, covers only part of the requirement, or lacks analysis.

**Rate "Not evident":** Only activity counts (events held, forms submitted, sessions conducted) without outcome measures. Or no data at all.

## Review — evaluation for continual improvement

**"Evident" requires ALL THREE:**
1. A formal review took place (meeting minutes, review report, management decision record)
2. The review specifically examined whether the approach and processes remain appropriate and effective
3. At least one specific improvement was identified and acted on — not just "reviewed and found satisfactory"

**Rate "Not evident" when:**
- No review records exist
- Review minutes exist but contain no substantive findings or improvement actions
- The "review" is a blanket annual sign-off with no documented analysis
- Only the strategic plan is reviewed but not the operational processes under examination
