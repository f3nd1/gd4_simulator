# Consultant Insights — What Leading PEIs Do Differently

Use this when making strategic recommendations beyond gap identification. The goal is to move the institution from compliance to genuine quality — a distinction CPE assessors can recognise and a Star rating requires.

## The compliance vs quality divide

Compliance PEIs ask: "What do we need to have to pass?"
Quality PEIs ask: "Does our system actually produce good student outcomes?"

The difference shows in the evidence:
- A compliance PEI has the policy; a quality PEI has the policy AND uses it to make decisions.
- A compliance PEI holds an annual survey; a quality PEI tracks the trend, shares results with students, and shows what changed as a result.
- A compliance PEI files the audit report; a quality PEI writes a management response that references specific improvements made.

## What leading PEIs do that average ones don't

**1. Policy ownership is real, not nominal.**
Every policy has a named owner (not "the institution") who is responsible for reviewing it on a fixed schedule. The owner's name appears on the document. This is visible evidence that the system is maintained by a person, not just filed away.

**2. The Academic Board makes substantive decisions.**
Leading PEIs have Academic Board minutes showing actual debate — a course that was changed because of student feedback, an assessment weighting revised because pass rates were out of range, a new programme requirement approved with documented rationale. A rubber-stamp board is a compliance gap; a working board is a quality asset.

**3. Review produces a feedback loop, not a report.**
The review cycle at a leading PEI follows: data → analysis → decision → action → follow-up. Minutes or reports show what changed as a result. "No issues found" is not an acceptable review conclusion unless accompanied by the data that confirmed no issues.

**4. Outcome data is triangulated.**
One metric is gaming-susceptible. Strong PEIs cross-check: graduation rate + re-sit rate + employer feedback + student satisfaction. If graduation rates are high but employer satisfaction is declining, that's a signal the outcomes are not as strong as they appear.

**5. Staff capability is proactively managed.**
Leading PEIs don't wait for a complaint to discover a teaching quality problem. They use peer observation, student mid-course feedback, and appraisal scores to identify at-risk situations early. The paper trail for this proactive management is what Band 5 evidence looks like.

## Strategic priorities by band level

**Currently Band 2 → targeting Band 3:**
Focus on completeness and consistency first. Get every required record produced for every required instance. Don't try to build a sophisticated review system yet — just make the base cycle run reliably. The biggest bang: assign explicit policy owners and put recurring reminders in place.

**Currently Band 3 → targeting Band 4:**
The review cycle is the key investment. Take one process (pick the most important or the one with the most data) and run a complete cycle: collect data → analyse → decide → act → record the improvement. Document this one cycle thoroughly. That documented improvement is the Band 4 evidence for that item.

**Currently Band 4 → targeting Band 5 (Star):**
Benchmarking is the unlock. Find an external reference point — industry employment rate data, graduate survey norms from a sector body, CPE published benchmarks. Show that you measure your outcomes against this external standard, and show what you do when you fall below it. Second Star lever: demonstrate that your system runs the same way even when key staff leave — what is the documented handover protocol, and has it been tested?

## Phrases that signal genuine quality

Use these to calibrate your read of evidence:

- "We reviewed student feedback from Semester 1 and identified that [specific issue]; we changed [specific thing] for Semester 2 and the repeat survey showed [improvement]." — Band 4 loop, well-evidenced.
- "Our graduate employment rate is benchmarked against the [sector] industry average published by [body]; we track our variance quarterly." — Band 5 benchmarking.
- "Following the departure of [staff member], the new [role] was onboarded using the documented procedure within [X] days with no process gaps." — Band 5 resilience.

Absence of this specificity — generic statements about commitment to quality, references to "our culture" — means you are looking at Brand 3 aspiration, not Band 4 or 5 evidence.
