# Fee Protection Scheme (FPS) rules (Criterion 4.2.2)

FPS is the mechanism that protects fees students have PAID if the PEI fails. It is gate-relevant and zero-tolerance: coverage gaps are regulatory breaches, not documentation niceties. This PEI has a live 2025 AFI in exactly this area — use its pattern as a template for what to check.

## The two FPS forms

- **Insurance scheme**: the PEI holds a master policy with an approved insurer; each student's paid fees are insured, evidenced by a per-student **FPS certificate/record** naming the student, the covered amount and the period.
- **Escrow scheme**: fees are paid into an escrow account with an approved bank and released to the PEI per the agreed schedule.
The PPD must state WHICH scheme is adopted and the operating procedure for it (who buys/extends cover, when, reconciliation steps, records kept).

## Coverage completeness — the core audit test

- **Every enrolled fee-paying student, for the full amount paid, for the full period.** The audit test is a reconciliation: fee receipts/enrolment register ↔ FPS records. Report it numerically: "X of Y sampled fee-paying students had FPS records matching the fees paid; Z had gaps of $N / date gaps."
- Timing: protection must be in place when the fee is collected — an FPS record dated after the receipt is a coverage gap for the interim period. Compare dates whenever both are visible.
- **Insurance-limit monitoring (this PEI's 2025 AFI, B18):** where the insurance scheme has a maximum insurable limit, the PPD must include **monitoring the utilisation of the coverage limit and the procedure for increasing the maximum insurable limit** before it is breached. A PPD that describes buying policies but never monitoring aggregate utilisation is incomplete — raise it.

## Publication duties (also from this PEI's 2025 AFI)

The PEI's own PPD commits to publishing FPS information. Check the evidence shows:
- a copy of the **FPS insurance certificate on the website**, and
- the **details of the adopted FPS in BOTH the student handbook and the website**.
If the PPD promises these and the evidence doesn't show them (screenshots, handbook pages), that is a "not implemented in accordance with its documented PPD" finding — quote the PPD promise and name what was absent.

## Interaction with fee collection

- The amount of fees a PEI may collect in advance is tied to its EduTrust status and FPS arrangements — the PPD must state the fee-collection cap the PEI operates under, and receipts must show collections within it. Where the PPD, the contract schedule and actual receipts imply different collection horizons, flag the inconsistency and name the documents (do not guess the statutory cap — test the PEI's own stated cap against its own records).

## Deferment / transfer / refund interactions

- Deferment: protection must continue (or be re-established) for fees already paid — the PPD must say how (a 2025 gap at this PEI for FPS-G in deferment cases).
- Refunds: when fees are refunded, FPS records should reflect the reduced exposure; when a transfer creates a new contract, cover must follow the new enrolment.

## How to audit

1. Identify the adopted scheme from the PPD; verify the operating procedure is documented end-to-end (purchase/extension trigger → per-student record → reconciliation → limit monitoring → publication).
2. Reconcile a sample: receipts ↔ FPS records (names, amounts, dates). State the counts.
3. Verify the publication evidence (website screenshot dated in-period, handbook page).
4. Any promise the PPD makes about FPS that the records don't evidence → "It was not evident that the PEI had [promise], in accordance with its documented PPD. Example: …".
