# Interview & Fieldwork — How an Auditor Probes Beyond Documents

Documents show what was written. Interviews reveal what actually happens. Use this skill to flag claims that need oral corroboration, note where an oral assertion substitutes for missing evidence, and write findings the way an experienced on-site auditor would.

## The "assert vs evidence" distinction

Everything a staff member says in an interview is an **assertion**, not evidence. It can corroborate a document (raise confidence) or contradict it (a finding), but it cannot substitute for it. When documents are absent, note: "Management asserted [claim] but no documentary evidence was sighted to corroborate this assertion."

This distinction matters for banding: a verbal explanation of a process, however detailed, cannot raise the Processes or Systems & Outcomes dimension above "Not evident" if no records exist.

## Standard probing questions by APSR dimension

**Approach (policy/procedure gaps):**
- "Who is responsible for maintaining this procedure? When was it last updated?"
- "How does a new staff member learn how to do this? Is there a documented SOP?"
- "Has this policy been formally approved? By whom and when?"
- Probe: if the document version date is suspicious (e.g. created one week before audit), ask "What process existed before this version?"

**Processes (implementation gaps):**
- "Can you show me a completed example of this record from the last six months?"
- "How many students went through this process in the last intake? Can we sample [N] files?"
- "What happens if a step is missed — who notices? What is the escalation path?"
- Probe: if a register is complete but dates cluster suspiciously around the audit date, note it.

**Systems & Outcomes (data gaps):**
- "What does success look like for this process? How do you know it's working?"
- "What is your target for [metric]? Where is that target documented and reviewed?"
- "What did you do differently after the last review of this outcome?"
- Probe: if outcomes are cited verbally without reference to a report or dashboard, ask "Where is this data recorded? May I see the report?"

**Review (improvement loop gaps):**
- "When was this process or outcome last formally reviewed? May I see the minutes or review record?"
- "What improvement action came out of that review? How was its effectiveness verified?"
- Probe: if review is claimed but no minutes exist, note "Management stated a review occurred; no written record was sighted."

## Flags to raise in findings

1. **Oral-only process**: "The process described by [role] has no documented SOP counterpart — knowledge is held individually, creating a single-person dependency and a sustainability risk (GD4 §[item])."
2. **Contradictory assertion**: "The [document] states [X]; however, [role] confirmed that in practice [Y]. The discrepancy suggests the documented procedure is not implemented as written."
3. **Post-audit documentation**: "The [document] version date is [date — within 4 weeks of audit]. The auditor was unable to verify whether this document was in place during the review period."
4. **No corroboration for outcome claim**: "Management cited an employment rate of [X]% but no survey instrument, response list or verified data source was provided. The claim is unsubstantiated and cannot be used to substantiate a Criterion 7 outcome."
5. **Key-person dependency**: "The described process depends entirely on [role]. No backup, documented handover procedure, or supervisory check was evidenced — a single point of failure."

## Writing the finding as an on-site auditor would

Lead with what was physically sighted (or not sighted), then note any oral assertions, then state the gap. Example:
"Auditor reviewed 10 randomly selected student files from the Jan–Jun 2025 cohort. Pre-course counselling forms (Form ADM-05) were absent from 7 of 10 files. The Admissions Manager stated that counselling was conducted but not always recorded on the form. No alternative record of counselling (e.g. email confirmation, meeting note) was sighted for the 7 affected students."
