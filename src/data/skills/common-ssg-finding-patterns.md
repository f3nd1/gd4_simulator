# Common SSG finding patterns (from this PEI's real 2025 & 2026 assessments)

These are the ACTUAL gap patterns SSG assessors raised at this PEI across two consecutive EduTrust assessments (35 AFIs in 2025, 19 in 2026). Audit with these patterns in mind — they are what real assessors look for here, and a repeat of a prior-year pattern is more serious than a new one.

## Pattern 1 — "Not documented in PPD" (dominant in 2026: 15 of 19 AFIs)

The PPD is missing a required PROCESS, not merely thin. Assessors enumerate the missing sub-parts as (a)/(b)/(c) lists. Test each Describe/Show requirement clause-by-clause and, when partially covered, name exactly which sub-parts are absent. Recurring blind spots at this PEI: processes for *analysing/using* data (not just collecting it — staff data analysis, survey findings feeding review), monitoring-of-staff processes (counsellors, admissions staff, under-performing academic staff), evaluation-of-effectiveness steps (support services, intervention measures, innovations), and completeness of enumerated lists (agent contract clauses, course review inputs, course planning elements, progress-report categories).

## Pattern 2 — "Not implemented in accordance with its documented PPD" (dominant in 2025: ~23 of 35)

The PPD promises a mechanism; the records don't show it happening. The register: "It was not evident that the PEI had [done X], in accordance with its documented PPD. Example: [named person/document/date]." Real examples here: appraisal system documented but not run for academic staff; peer reviews promised but absent; part-time staff excluded from the staff survey the PPD includes them in; assignments not moderated and papers not approved by the Examination Board; invigilators not briefed (named individuals); intervention measures never evaluated. **Treat every PPD promise as a named check** — verify each against records, and cite the specific missing record.

## Pattern 3 — Internal contradiction inside the PPD

Two different values for the same commitment (2025: feedback tracked "within 5 working days" in one place, "within 3 working days" in another). Whenever the same number/timeline/owner appears twice, compare the instances verbatim.

## Pattern 4 — Cross-document mismatch (PPD ↔ contract ↔ website/registry)

The highest-value single check at this PEI: the refund/cooling-off mismatch between PPD and Student Contract Clause 3.8 was raised in BOTH 2025 and 2026 (an un-remediated repeat). Others: communication channels not matching the Stakeholder Registry in the SMS; FPS details promised for handbook+website but absent. Always extract commitments from each document separately, then diff them.

## Pattern 5 — No timeline / no monitoring

Approved actions without execution timelines (2026: "17 out of 19 approved follow-up actions" from Management Review 2025 had no timelines — a countable, named example); CAP implementation not monitored; review frequency never stated; feedback cycle "reviewed periodically" instead of a defined cycle. Wherever an action list or review commitment appears, count how many entries carry dates/owners and report the fraction.

## Pattern 6 — Named-example discipline (what makes findings stick)

Real assessor negatives cite concrete artefacts: a teacher's contract signed 8 Aug 2024 for a deployment that ended 31 Jan 2024; three PPDs stuck at V0 with dates; a named student without a Form 12 acknowledgement; a letter of appointment acknowledged by the wrong person; one person appointed to all four assessment roles. Emulate this: dates compared, versions quoted, people/documents named, counts stated ("17 out of 19"). A negative without a named example is not assessor-grade.

## Independence & document control (Criterion 6/2 recurring)

- Internal assessment staffed by people independent of the area assessed (2025 AFI) — check who audited what against who owns what.
- Document control: revision history + approval authority actually maintained (2025 AFI cited three policies at V0). Documents that never move past V0 across years indicate control exists on paper only.
