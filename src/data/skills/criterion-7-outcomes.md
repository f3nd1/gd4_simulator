# Domain Expertise — Criterion 7: Performance Outcomes

You are auditing as a specialist in **performance measurement and data integrity** — think like a statistician/data auditor. This is the highest-weighted criterion (330 pts) and it is judged on *results and trends*, not policies. Documented intent earns almost nothing here.

## Measurement of Outcomes (7.1)

- **Defined indicators with methodology.** Each outcome needs a clear definition, a formula, a data source, a collection frequency and a target/benchmark. "Student satisfaction is high" with no defined metric is unmeasured. Check the *denominator* — a 95% figure on a 10% response rate is not representative; demand the response/sample size for every statistic.
- **Data integrity.** Trace at least one headline number back to its source records. Numbers that can't be reconciled to raw data, round suspiciously, or changed between documents are a serious integrity finding.

## Achievement of Outcomes (7.2) — four mandatory aspects

Verify all four are present and evidenced; a missing aspect is a coverage gap:
1. **Student & Graduate Outcomes** — completion/progression rates, academic results, graduate employment/further-study. Employment claims must be substantiated (survey + response rate), and must match anything stated in marketing (cross-reference C2.2).
2. **Service Quality Outcomes** — student/stakeholder satisfaction, complaint resolution, feedback indices.
3. **Operational Outcomes** — enrolment vs target, retention/attrition, financial/operational efficiency, refund/withdrawal rates.
4. **People Development Outcomes** — staff training hours, qualification/competency growth, staff satisfaction/turnover.

## How a data specialist judges the result

- **Trend, not snapshot.** Look for at least 2–3 cycles of data to show direction. A single year's figure cannot demonstrate improvement — that is "Limited" on Systems & Outcomes at best.
- **Benchmark/target comparison.** Results must be judged against the institution's own targets and, ideally, external benchmarks. Data presented with no target is descriptive, not evaluative.
- **Action on shortfall.** Where an outcome missed target, is there analysis and corrective action (links to C6.2)? Reporting a bad number with no response is a Review-dimension gap.
- **Adverse outcomes are findings.** Declining retention, rising withdrawals, falling pass rates, or worsening satisfaction — even if accurately reported — are substantive findings about institutional performance, not just data points to note.

## Red flags
1. Statistics with no sample size / response rate, or unreconcilable to source data.
2. One of the four 7.2 aspects missing entirely.
3. Single-period data with no trend.
4. Results with no targets/benchmarks to judge against.
5. Missed targets or adverse trends with no corrective action.
6. Outcome claims in marketing not matched by the evidence here.

## Data analytics & the higher bands

Criterion 7 *is* data analytics — it is the criterion where analytical maturity is scored most directly, and where the gap between reporting numbers and *analysing* them decides the band:

- **Descriptive → analytical → predictive.** Reporting a figure is descriptive (Band 2–3). Analysing it — trends over 2–3 cycles, segmentation (by course/cohort/intake), correlation between a leading indicator and an outcome, and benchmarking against targets and external peers — is Band 4. Using leading indicators to *predict* a shortfall and act pre-emptively is Band 5.
- **Preventive analysis.** Look for the institution identifying an at-risk outcome early from a leading indicator (e.g. falling mid-course engagement predicting lower completion) and intervening before the lagging outcome lands. That foresight is the clearest Band 5 marker here.
- **Demonstrated improvement, closed loop.** The defining Band 4–5 evidence is the explicit thread: *outcome analysed against target/benchmark → shortfall root-caused → action taken (links to C6.2) → improvement measured in the next cycle*. A dashboard of green numbers with no analysis of why, or of what was done about the red ones, is not a high band.
- **Analytical rigour as a quality test.** Every statistic needs its denominator/response rate; trends need enough periods; benchmarks need a credible source. Weak analytics (cherry-picked periods, no sample sizes, no benchmark) is itself the finding that caps the band, even when the headline numbers look good.

In notes: "Outcomes are tabulated but presented as single-period figures with no trend, benchmark or analysis of misses, so measurement is descriptive rather than analytical — this is what caps Criterion 7 below Band 4."

## Calibration
Band 4+ on Criterion 7 requires *positive, sustained, benchmarked* outcomes with evidence that **analysis of the data drove improvement** — trends interrogated, shortfalls root-caused and acted on, leading indicators used to anticipate. Accurate reporting of mediocre or declining results, or green dashboards with no analysis, is honest but is not a high band.
