# SSG refund, withdrawal and cooling-off rules (Criterion 4.3 / 4.4)

You are auditing the PEI's refund and withdrawal machinery. This is one of the most reliably-cited AFI areas in real EduTrust assessments — including this PEI's own past reports — because the rules live in THREE documents that must agree word-for-word: the PPD, the Standard Student Contract (SSC), and the public-facing material (website / student handbook).

## The cooling-off rule (the single most common refund AFI)

- The SSC provides a **cooling-off period of at least 7 working days after signing the contract**. Withdrawal within the cooling-off period entitles the student to a refund of **ALL Course Fees and Miscellaneous Fees paid** — a FULL refund, NOT the "highest percentage in Schedule D".
- **Known failure pattern (raised against this PEI in both 2025 and 2026):** the PPD states cooling-off withdrawal gives "the highest refund percentage in Schedule D" (e.g. max 90%) while the contract's cooling-off clause (Clause 3.8 in this PEI's SSC) requires a full refund. **These are different amounts. Any PPD that routes cooling-off refunds through Schedule D percentages is WRONG and is an automatic AFI.** Check the exact wording.

## Refund for withdrawal WITHOUT cause (Schedule D)

- Schedule D of the SSC is the PEI's own committed refund table: percentage of fees refunded, banded by how far before/after Course Commencement the written notice is received.
- Audit checks: (a) the percentages and time bands in the PPD **match Schedule D digit-for-digit**; (b) the same table appears consistently in the student handbook / website; (c) the PPD states **how the computed refund amount is communicated to the student** (a documented computation-communication step — its absence was raised against this PEI in both reports); (d) worked refund computations in the records actually apply the correct band (compare the notice date to the band used).

## Refund for withdrawal WITH cause

- Where the PEI fails to start/continue the course, changes it materially, loses registration, or misrepresents — the student is entitled to a full refund of unconsumed fees within the contractual deadline. Check the PPD lists these triggers and the timeline.

## Refund processing deadline

- The SSC commits the PEI to pay refunds within a fixed number of working days of receiving the refund request (7 working days is the standard SSC commitment). Check: PPD deadline = SSC deadline = handbook/website deadline, and test actual refund records against it (request date → payment date).

## Transfer / deferment / withdrawal process (4.3)

- **Transfer = a new contract.** An approved course transfer requires terminating the old student contract and **executing a new SSC** for the new course. A documented contract-execution step for approved transfers must exist in the PPD (its absence was a 2025 AFI for this PEI).
- **Deferment and fee protection:** the PPD must state how fees remain protected under the adopted FPS during a deferment (protection must not silently lapse while the student is deferred — also a 2025 AFI here).
- Student's Pass holders: transfer/withdrawal procedures must address ICA notification obligations.
- Timelines: the PPD must state the processing time for transfer/deferment/withdrawal decisions and evidence must show cases decided within it.

## How to audit this area

1. Extract every refund/withdrawal number (percentages, day counts, deadlines) from the PPD, and separately from the SSC/handbook text in the evidence.
2. Compare pairwise. **Any mismatch between PPD and SSC is a finding on its own** ("cross-document mismatch") — quote both passages with their chunk IDs.
3. For implementation: sample actual refund/withdrawal cases — notice date, band applied, amount, payment date — and verify against the committed table and deadline.
4. Never accept "refunds are handled per the student contract" as a documented process: the PPD itself must state the mechanics (who computes, how communicated, deadline, records kept).
