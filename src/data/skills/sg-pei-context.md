# Singapore PEI Regulatory Context

## The regulatory landscape

Private Education Institutions (PEIs) in Singapore are regulated by SkillsFuture Singapore (SSG). SSG took over the regulation of the private education sector after the dissolution of the Committee for Private Education (CPE), which was previously a statutory board under SSG. EduTrust certification is mandatory for PEIs enrolling international students. The certification comes in three tiers: **Provisional** (new applicants), **4-Year**, and **4-Year (Star)** — the Star rating is the highest and hardest to achieve.

GD4 is the fourth generation of the EduTrust Guidance Document. It replaced GD3 in 2025 and places significantly heavier weight on outcomes (Criterion 7, 330 points) and on evidence of systematic review for continual improvement.

## Student Protection Framework — Criterion 4 requirements in context

The following are regulatory hard requirements (not just best practice) that affect what evidence must exist:

- **Student Contract (4.2):** Every student must have a signed contract on the SSG-prescribed template. Deviations from the template require SSG approval. The contract must be signed BEFORE fees are collected. Missing or post-dated contracts are a regulatory breach, not just an audit gap.
- **Fee Protection Scheme (4.2):** All student fees must be protected under either the SSG-approved bank insurance or escrow scheme. Evidence should include FPS certificates per student and bank/escrow records. "We use GIRO" is not FPS compliance.
- **Refund Policy (4.4):** SSG prescribes a mandatory minimum refund table. The institution's refund policy must meet or exceed the SSG table. Evidence must show actual refunds were processed per policy, not just that the policy exists.
- **Pre-Course Counselling (4.1):** SSG requires documented pre-course counselling before enrolment. The record must show the student was informed of: course details, entry requirements, fees and FPS, withdrawal/refund policy, and student support services. A tick-box form is weak; a signed counselling checklist with student acknowledgement is stronger.

## What "good" evidence looks like in Singapore context

**For a small-to-medium PEI (50–500 students):**
- Meeting minutes: Quarterly board/management meetings minimum. Minutes should reference specific agenda items (not just "general business"), record decisions with rationale, and assign action items with owners and due dates.
- Feedback systems: An annual student satisfaction survey is the minimum. Band 4–5 institutions use semester-level surveys with trend analysis and demonstrate that feedback has changed something.
- Staff appraisal: Annual appraisal for all staff (academic and non-academic). Academic Board must have approved minimum qualifications for academic roles — the Academic Board minutes should show this discussion.
- Financial controls: SSG requires annual external audit. The audit report should be available; management responses to audit findings should be documented.

## Common gaps seen in Singapore PEIs (patterns to flag)

1. **"We are a small school" gap:** Small institutions often have undocumented informal processes ("everyone knows what to do"). Flag this specifically — informality is not a size exemption under EduTrust.
2. **The academic-board-on-paper gap:** An Academic Board that meets once a year with generic minutes and no substantive curriculum/assessment decisions. The Academic Board is required to be involved in course design, assessment policies, and academic staff recruitment. If minutes show only attendance and a rubber-stamp of management decisions, this is Beginning at best.
3. **The one-person system gap:** Key processes (fee collection, contract management, student records) that depend entirely on one staff member with no documented process. Highly vulnerable to staff turnover — flag as sustainability concern.
4. **Post-audit documentation:** Documents created or significantly updated in the month before the audit. Look for suspiciously recent version dates on policies that should have been in place for years.
5. **Criterion 7 outcomes weakness:** Many PEIs track graduation rates and employment rates as required, but do not analyse trends or benchmark against targets. Outcome data collected but not analysed = Processes without Outcomes. The GD4 scoring places 330 points on Criterion 7 — it is the single largest criterion.

## EduTrust band language (use when giving higher-band advice)

- "To move toward a 4-Year (Star) rating, the institution needs to demonstrate…"
- "This gap is likely to result in a Provisional or conditional certification unless addressed"
- Reference the specific GD4 criterion and item number (e.g. "GD4 4.2.1") rather than generic "EduTrust requirements"
- The assessor (SSG) will use the same APSR rubric — your AI verdicts simulate what an SSG EduTrust assessor would find
