# Benchmarking & Good Practice — What High-Band Looks Like

When writing the "higher band" recommendation, name a concrete target, not a platitude. "Improve the review process" is not guidance. This skill gives the AI the reference points to write recommendations that an institution can actually act on.

## Band calibration benchmarks

**Band 2 (Basic compliance):** Policy exists, records exist for some cases. No trend data, no benchmarks, no review loop. Institution can describe what it does but cannot show it works.

**Band 3 (Consistent compliance):** Documented procedures, consistent records across the review period. Some outcome data collected. Review occurs but outputs are observations, not decisions. This is the EduTrust minimum for most sub-criteria.

**Band 4 (Review, Learn and Adjust):** Data drives decisions. Trends over 2–3 cycles reviewed at management level. Shortfalls root-caused with specific corrective actions that closed the gap. A stakeholder-informed improvement is traceable end to end. External benchmark used to contextualise internal data.

**Band 5 (Anticipate, Prevent, Stability):** Leading indicators are monitored and used to intervene before lagging outcomes deteriorate. Improvement is sustained across cycles. The institution actively seeks external comparators and uses scenario analysis. Preventive action is triggered by data, not by a crisis.

## Concrete Band 4–5 examples by criterion

**C1 — Governance/Finance:** Board receives monthly dashboard with actual vs budget, trend line, and 3-month rolling cash projection. Variance > 10% triggers a board paper with root cause and recovery plan. Last cycle: identified a fee-collection timing risk in Q2, renegotiated payment terms, outcome verified at Q3 board meeting.

**C2 — HR/Marketing:** Staff turnover tracked quarterly by department; exit-interview themes coded and reviewed at management meeting. One cycle: identified that two teaching staff left citing workload — academic workload model revised, turnover in that department fell from 30% to 10% next cycle. Marketing claim accuracy checked by comparing enrolment-stage claim against graduation-stage actuals.

**C3 — Agents:** Per-agent scorecard with withdrawal rate, complaint count, and student academic performance tracked each intake. Threshold breach (>15% withdrawal attributable to one agent) triggers a performance-improvement notice within 30 days. One agent placed on probation after data trigger; retrained and re-evaluated; metrics improved; decision documented.

**C4 — Student Protection:** Attendance data fed into a weekly at-risk report. Students below 85% (pre-threshold) receive proactive contact; those below 75% (ICA threshold) are immediately escalated. Outcome: early-warning interventions in 2024 reduced Student Pass revocations from 8 to 2. Withdrawal reason coded and trended: identified that 60% of voluntary withdrawals cited "course expectations not as described in counselling"; counselling script revised; withdrawal rate fell next intake.

**C5 — Academic:** Grade distribution and pass rate for each module reviewed at end of each intake by Academic Board. Item analysis conducted for any module with pass rate below 70% — identifies which questions/criteria most students failed. One module redesigned after item analysis showed 80% failure on a specific assessment task; pass rate improved from 63% to 84% next intake. At-risk students (attendance + formative grades) identified by Week 4 and placed on an academic support plan.

**C6 — Quality Assurance:** Internal audit covers all 24 sub-criteria over the 4-year cycle with findings tracked in a live NC register. Management Review meets quarterly; agenda always includes: audit NC status, KPI dashboard (all 7 criteria), feedback themes, and resource review. One cycle: management review spotted a rising trend in student complaints about class scheduling → process change → complaints fell 40% next semester, verified at next management review.

**C7 — Outcomes:** Employment rate tracked for each graduate cohort with verified survey methodology (>70% response rate). Benchmarked against SSG sector average and the institution's own 3-year target. One cohort fell 8% below target; root-cause analysis identified a weak industry-placement module; module redesigned; next cohort employment rate recovered and exceeded target.

## Language for "higher band" recommendations

Instead of: "Improve data collection and analysis."
Write: "To achieve Band 4 on this sub-criterion, the institution should: (1) establish a formal [metric] dashboard reviewed at [meeting] quarterly; (2) document the target for [metric] in the strategic plan and show comparison at each review; (3) produce one traceable improvement story — a shortfall root-caused, action taken, outcome measured in the next cycle — and include it in the Management Review minutes."

Instead of: "Benchmark against peers."
Write: "Identify one external benchmark for [metric] — SSG sector data, an industry body survey, or a comparable PEI's published outcome — and use it as the upper bound against which the institution's own result is judged. Present this comparison at the next Academic Board or Management Review meeting."
