# Finding Specificity — How to Write an Audit Finding

A finding is a formal record that must stand alone. Someone reading it six months later — an SSG assessor, a Board member, a new principal — must be able to understand exactly what the gap was, where it was found, how widespread it is, and why it matters, without any oral explanation from the auditor. Vague findings are unacceptable.

## The five-part finding structure

Every finding must contain all five parts:

**1. Title** — One line identifying the gap. Format: `GD4 [item] — [Gap in plain English]`
Example: `GD4 4.2.1 — Pre-Course Counselling Records Missing for Jan–Jun 2025 Cohort`

**2. Observation (the Condition)** — What the auditor found. This is the most important part. See specificity rules below.

**3. Criteria** — What the GD4 standard requires. Quote or closely paraphrase the exact GD4 requirement. Never write "EduTrust requires…" without the item number.
Example: `GD4 4.2.1 requires documented pre-course counselling for every student before enrolment, covering course details, fees, FPS arrangement, and withdrawal/refund policy.`

**4. Effect / Risk** — Why the gap matters. Name the concrete consequence: regulatory risk, student harm, certification impact. Never leave this blank.
Example: `Without these records, the institution cannot demonstrate to SSG that students were informed of their contractual rights before committing to fees. This is a Category A regulatory requirement; incomplete records expose the institution to enforcement action and will cap this criterion at Band 2 or below.`

**5. Root cause, corrective action, preventive action** — covered separately in finding-writing.md.

## The observation statement — specificity rules

The observation is the core of the finding. It MUST include:

| Element | Weak (reject) | Strong (require) |
|---|---|---|
| **Who** | "staff did not…" | Name the role (or name if known): "the Admissions Counsellor (Ms Lim, appointed Jan 2024)" |
| **What** | "records are missing" | Name the specific document: "Form ADM-05 (Pre-Course Counselling Record)" |
| **When** | "recently" / "last year" | Exact period: "for the Jan–Jun 2025 intake, reviewed on 15 Jun 2025" |
| **How many** | "several" / "some" / "many" | Exact fraction: "8 of 23 student files reviewed" — always state the denominator |
| **Which** | "a number of students" | Identify the cohort, course, or specific students where possible: "all 8 are international students enrolled in the Diploma in Business Management" |
| **GD4 reference** | "the requirement" | Specific clause: "GD4 4.2.1" not just "4.2" or "Criterion 4" |

**Never write:** "Documentation is incomplete / insufficient / missing."
**Always write:** "Form ADM-05 was absent from 8 of 23 student intake files reviewed (Jan–Jun 2025 cohort, Diploma in Business Management). The 8 missing files all relate to students enrolled between 1 April and 15 June 2025."

## Observation templates by dimension

**Procedure (Approach) gap:**
"The [document name] (version [X], dated [date]) does not address [specific requirement]. Specifically, [clause/section] states [current text], which does not meet GD4 [item] because [what is missing]. No earlier version of this document was provided."

**Evidence (Processes) gap:**
"[Record type] was not found for [N] of [total] [scope reviewed] (covering [period]). The [N] missing records relate to [describe who/what). The most recent record on file is dated [date]. [Staff role name] confirmed during review that [brief explanation if given]."

**Outcomes gap:**
"The institution has not collected / analysed [metric type] for [period]. Activity data (e.g. [example]) is available but no outcome data (i.e. [what outcome should look like]) was provided. No trend analysis covering more than one cycle was found."

**Review gap:**
"No formal review of [process/policy name] has been conducted since [date, or 'the policy was established in [year]']. [Meeting name] minutes from [period reviewed] do not contain any reference to this process. The Management Review agenda template (provided) does not include a standing item for reviewing [topic]."

## Severity language to use in the Effect section

**Regulatory breach (use for Category A gaps):**
"This is a mandatory SSG requirement under GD4 [item]. Non-compliance cannot be offset by strong performance elsewhere. The institution must rectify this before the assessment date; SSG may treat an unresolved breach as a separate enforcement matter."

**Star-disqualifying (use for Criterion 7 or governance gaps):**
"Criterion 7 carries 330 of the 1,000 available points. A Band 2 result on this sub-criterion makes a 4-Year (Star) outcome arithmetically unachievable regardless of performance in other criteria."

**Band-capping (use for evidence or review gaps):**
"Under the EduTrust APSR rubric, a [Processes / Review] dimension rated 'Not evident' caps this sub-criterion at Band [2/3] regardless of the strength of the documented approach. Resolving this gap is a prerequisite for any band above [current cap]."

## Common mistakes in finding writing

1. **Passive voice hides the owner.** "Records were not maintained" — by whom? Write "The Admissions team did not maintain records of…"
2. **Hedging weakens the finding.** "It appears that…" / "It seems possible that…" — state what you found. If you are uncertain, say "The auditor was unable to locate [record] in the files provided."
3. **Scope creep.** One finding = one gap. If you found three gaps in sub-criterion 4.2, raise three findings, each with its own observation, criteria, and actions.
4. **Effect is missing.** "No review was conducted" — so what? Always state the regulatory, certification, or operational consequence.
5. **Corrective action is vague.** "Management to improve processes" is not an action. "Admissions Manager to complete Form ADM-05 for all [N] affected students by [date] and attach to their student files" is an action.
